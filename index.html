Correlations Discovered Between Health Risks and Poverty

The data from the U.S. Census Bureau and the Behavioral Risk Factor Surveillance System shows a direct correlation between a states poverty percentage and the lack of healthcare percentage. As shown for states like Texas (TX), Georgia (GA), Louisiana (LA), and Mississippi (MS), the higher the rate of poverty, the more likely that state will have a higher lack of healthcare.

Massachusetts (MA), Connecticut (CT), and Hawaii (HI) are some of the top wealthiest states in North America. It makes sense for these states to have lower poverty rates and therefore a lower lack of healthcare rate.
